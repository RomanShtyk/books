The Staff Engineer's Path by Tanya Reilly

### Intro

Three pillars of the staff position are:

* Big Picture
* Execution
* Leveling Up Team

## Part I

### The Big Picture

Teams without cross-team leadership make excellent local maximum decisions and software projects get stuck

Scope = the domain/teams you're responsible for and influential in, regardless of formal authority.
Within your scope you should:

* Influence short- and long-term goals
* Stay aware of major decisions and advocate for those without leverage
* Mentor and grow the next generation of senior/staff engineers

  Scope too broad → risks:
* No focused impact; endless side quests with no narrative
* You become a bottleneck others can't work without
* Decision fatigue from constantly triaging what to work on
* Shallow relationships; no one gets a "local" staff engineer to mentor them

  Scope too narrow → risks:
* Wasted expertise on work that doesn't need staff-level skill
* Opportunity cost; invisible to the rest of the org
* Overshadowing junior engineers, taking their growth opportunities
* Overengineering simple problems because you're underutilized
